As we balance the risk and rewards of the future, we are tempted with the need to prepare especially when the percieved risks outweigh the rewards. Such a need is satisfied either through commercial avenues of prediction such as tarot card readings, through a practice of traditional superstition, or through guides which tell us how to be better prepared to deal with the outcomes of risk. In this process, we continually ask ourselves if the reward be so easily attained through such modes of preparedness? Moreover, when do we know if the reward is ascertained or not?

Perhaps, the answer is revealed to us not in a question of if or when. Maybe the answer is not revealed to us at all. Instead, the rewards of a life may come in the form of glimpses into the future—random moments which almost seem fateful and too impossible to comprehend. These coincidences tell us in an almost otherworldly way that participating in the journey outweighs the pressures we put in ensuring its end. From these abstractions of the future we can then find the poetry in the ordinary of a life. Or, more or less, push the boundaries of what we ascertain as the impossible from the possible.
