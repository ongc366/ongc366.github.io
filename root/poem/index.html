As we balance the risks and rewards of the future we are tempted with the need to prepare, especially when the risks seem to outweigh the rewards. From commercial avenues of prediction, like having your palm read, to the daily observance of traditional superstition, like avoiding black cats, we strive to make sense of the future and prepare for the worst. On the way, we then ask ourselves: if the reward be so easily attained through preparedness and when do we know if the reward can be ascertained or not?

Perhaps the answer lies not in asking questions of when or if. Perhaps the answer may never reveal itself. Instead what may be revealed to us is the reward of an unseen future masked behind an image of an ordinary life—coincidence. An alternative to real magic beyond prediction. A reward that is less of a question, but a random event that becomes clear and meaningful with just a shift in perspective. It tells us in an almost otherworldly way that participating in the journey may outweigh the pressures we put in ensuring its end. Moreover that the future cannot in fact be predicted, but appreciated. From this abstraction of the future we can then find the poetry in our life and begin to reap its rewards.
