Across the table.

To be honest, I think the whole concept of speed dating is really pretty silly. I mean does it really take the same amount of time to brew a cup of coffee as it does to find the love of your life? But, there must be a reason why it still very much a part of dating today. Perhaps, it is something beyond what science can explain, instead going into what science can create?

I realize now that what is really quite amazing about speed dating is the amount of trust and spontaneity put into the actual effort of finding. Although the one across the table from you may not be the love of your life, he or she is a reflection of your own vulnerability. Of innocence, giving up onself to fate, and the fun that comes with actualizing on hope (woah, I'm going to have to underline that one.)

As an ode to the spirit of speed dating, I have decided to show different vignettes of pretend dates. In each of these vignettes, there is a couple engaging in a little Q/A.
Each Q/A conversation is randomly separated in half. As the user pieces the conversation together by cycling through the program, the user is placed under the spontaneity that
comes with trust and the hope that comes with finding the one's other half.
